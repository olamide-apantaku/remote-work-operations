## Remote Work Setup
**Essential Equipment:**
- Stable and reliable internet connection
- Backup internet source — mobile data
- Dedicated and quiet workspace
- Headset for clear communication
- Second screen if possible for efficiency

**Essential Software:**
- CRM system — Zendesk, HubSpot or Freshdesk
- Communication — Slack, Zoom, Google Meet
- Task management — Notion, Trello, Monday.com
- Documentation — Google Workspace, Notion
- Time tracking — Clockify or similar tool

## Daily Routine and Structure

**Morning Routine:**
- Log in 10 minutes before shift starts
- Check all communication channels — Slack, email
- Review open tickets from previous shift
- Check and acknowledge handover notes
- Set daily priorities and ticket targets

**During Shift:**
- Respond to tickets within defined SLA windows
- Update ticket status after every interaction
- Communicate blockers to Team Lead immediately
- Take scheduled breaks to maintain focus
- Document all interactions in CRM in real time

**End of Shift:**
- Complete all pending ticket updates
- Prepare detailed handover notes
- Flag urgent or unresolved tickets to next agent
- Log off all systems securely
- Send end of shift report to Team Lead

## Communication Guidelines

**Response Time Expectations:**
| Channel | Response Time |
|---|---|
| Slack — urgent | Within 15 minutes |
| Slack — general | Within 1 hour |
| Email | Within 2 hours |
| Team meetings | Attend on time always |

**Communication Best Practices:**
- Always acknowledge messages even if 
  full response is not yet ready
- Use clear and concise language in all messages
- Flag blockers and issues early — never wait
- Keep Team Lead informed of workload and capacity
- Use proper channels for proper purposes — 
  urgent issues on Slack, updates on email
- Always be professional in written communication
- Use status updates on Slack to show availability

## Task and Ticket Management
- Prioritise tickets by urgency and SLA deadline
- Never leave a ticket without a status update
- Use CRM tags and categories consistently
- Review ticket queue at the start of every hour
- Escalate tickets that are outside your scope immediately
- Document every interaction before moving to next ticket
- Use saved templates and macros for efficiency
- Monitor own KPIs daily — CSAT, AHT, FCR, SLA

## Meeting and Collaboration
**Before Every Meeting:**
- Test audio and video connection
- Join 2 minutes early
- Have agenda and notes ready
- Mute when not speaking

**During Meetings:**
- Contribute actively and constructively
- Take notes on action items
- Ask questions clearly and concisely
- Stay focused — no multitasking

**After Meetings:**
- Document action items immediately
- Share notes with team on Slack or Notion
- Follow up on assigned tasks promptly
- Update Team Lead on progress

## Shift Handover Process
A proper handover prevents tickets from 
falling through the cracks between shifts.

**Handover Note Must Include:**
- All open tickets with current status
- Next steps for each unresolved ticket
- Urgent or sensitive cases flagged clearly
- Any customer callbacks or follow ups pending
- System issues or blockers encountered
- KPI summary for the shift

**Handover Template:**

    Date: [DATE]
    Agent: [NAME]
    Shift: [START TIME] to [END TIME]

    Open Tickets: [NUMBER]
    Resolved Tickets: [NUMBER]
    Escalated Tickets: [NUMBER]

    Urgent Cases:
    - [Ticket ID] — [Brief description] — [Next step]

    Follow Ups Required:
    - [Customer name] — [Issue] — [Due by]

    Notes for Next Agent:
    - [Any important information]

## Performance and Accountability
**Daily Self Check:**
- Am I meeting my SLA targets?
- Is my CSAT score on track?
- Are my tickets updated and documented?
- Have I communicated blockers to my Team Lead?
- Am I maintaining quality across all interactions?

**Weekly Review:**
| Metric | Target | My Performance |
|---|---|---|
| CSAT Score | 90%+ | Track weekly |
| SLA Compliance | 95-100% | Track weekly |
| First Contact Resolution | 70%+ | Track weekly |
| Average Handle Time | Reduce monthly | Track weekly |
| Tickets Resolved | Meet daily target | Track daily |

## Remote Work Best Practices
- Treat remote work with the same professionalism 
  as office work
- Maintain regular working hours and stick to them
- Communicate availability clearly on Slack
- Never go silent during working hours
- Build relationships with teammates deliberately — 
  remote work can feel isolating
- Take breaks away from the screen regularly
- Keep your workspace organised and distraction free
- Invest in your own development — certifications, 
  courses, reading
- Ask for feedback regularly and act on it
- Celebrate your wins — remote workers often go unnoticed

## Common Remote Work Challenges

| Challenge | Solution |
|---|---|
| Internet outage | Always have mobile data backup ready |
| Feeling isolated | Stay active on team channels and check ins |
| Distractions at home | Dedicated workspace and set boundaries |
| Communication gaps | Over communicate — update status regularly |
| Burnout | Strict start and end times, regular breaks |
| Missing context | Document everything, ask questions early |
| Time zone differences | Confirm meeting times in your local time always |
